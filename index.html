THE CREDIBILITY PROBLEM · stated plainly
Estimates for a single AI query range from 0.03 to 68 grams of CO₂e across published sources — a spread wide enough to be meaningless without stating assumptions. OpenAI and Google have released average per-query energy figures, but neither discloses the methodology behind them; Anthropic has published no Scope 1, 2 or 3 emissions in any public filing as of March 2026.

This dashboard does not resolve that uncertainty — it makes it visible. Every figure is labelled Measured Benchmarked or Estimated, with its source and coefficient traceable. A reviewer can disagree with an assumption and recompute, rather than accepting or rejecting a black-box total.
Why this matters commercially: as procurement questionnaires and Fortune 500 RFPs add mandatory sustainability scoring, "we cannot measure our AI footprint" becomes a lost-deal risk. A defensible, labelled estimate outperforms silence.
